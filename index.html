Costly beatings

In just three years, Baltimore City has paid out $5.7 million to compensate battered dozens of residents who suffered broken bones — jaws, noses, arms, legs, ankles — head trauma, organ failure, and even death, during questionable arrests. And that does not include millions of dollars paid out to lawyers in legal fees. Some residents were beaten while handcuffed; others were thrown to the pavement.
